UrbanRoll: 2024 Executive Insights

An analytical deep-dive into UrbanRoll, a bike-sharing service, focusing on user behavior, operational efficiency, and growth opportunities. This project analyzes data from over 15,000+ rides to optimize fleet management and user engagement.

📊 Key Findings

1. The "Double-Hump" Operational Trend
Analysis confirms UrbanRoll is primarily a commuting tool.
Peak Demand: Usage spikes significantly at 7:00 AM and 3:00 PM.
Strategic Move: "Rebalancing" crews (trucks moving bikes) should be most active between 10:00 AM and 2:00 PM to prepare for the evening rush.
2. User Personas: Subscriber vs. Casual
The Commuter (Subscriber): Short, predictable trips averaging 15 minutes. These users form the backbone of daily volume.
The Explorer (Casual): Longer, more erratic trips averaging 35 minutes. While lower in volume, they account for higher "bike time" usage.
Growth Strategy: Launch a "Weekend Explorer" pass specifically for casual users to boost off-peak frequency.
3. The Rebalancing Crisis
Using Net Flow CTE, we identified critical station types:
Sinks: Stations that end up with too many bikes (typically near business districts).
Sources: Stations that run out of bikes (typically near residential areas).
Proposed Solution: Implement "Dynamic Incentives"—offering discounts to users who end their rides at "Source" stations to help rebalance the fleet naturally.
4. Growth & Retention
The analysis of Month-over-Month (MoM) growth and user loyalty revealed two primary takeaways:
The Power User Core: A small group of "Power Users" (the top 1%) accounts for a disproportionate amount of total revenue.
Steady Expansion: By using a 7-day moving average to smooth out daily fluctuations ("noise"), the data confirms the company is growing at a consistent rate of X% month-over-month.

🛠 Tech & Methodology

Dataset: 15,000+ ride records.
Metric: Net Flow CTE for station logistics.
Focus Areas: Operational efficiency, user segmentation, and retention strategies.
